# Oil & Gas Project Typology — Civil Engineering Perspective

## Discipline Overview for Oil & Gas

This document provides the **Civil Engineering** perspective on oil & gas projects. It covers discipline-specific considerations, deliverables, compliance requirements, and workflows relevant to civil works, earthworks, drainage, roads, foundations, structural design, and civil infrastructure engineering.

## Key Discipline-Specific Considerations

### Sector-Specific Civil Engineering Requirements

| Aspect | Consideration | Impact on Civil Engineering |
|--------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Project Scale | Oil & Gas projects vary significantly in scale and complexity | Drives resource planning, team sizing, and level of effort |
| Regulatory Environment | Sector-specific regulations and standards | Determines compliance requirements and documentation needs |
| Stakeholder Profile | Owner, contractors, regulators, community | Influences communication protocols and reporting structures |
| Risk Profile | Technical, commercial, schedule, and safety risks | Shapes risk management approach and contingency planning |
| Quality Standards | Industry-specific quality requirements | Defines inspection, testing, and verification protocols |

### Typical Civil Engineering Deliverables

| Deliverable | Description | Project Phase |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Discipline-Specific Plan | Detailed execution plan for Civil Engineering activities | Planning / FEED |

### Compliance and Standards

| Standard / Regulation | Application | Jurisdiction |
|----------------------|-------------|--------------|

| Client specifications | Contractual compliance | Per contract |

### Civil Engineering Workflow Integration

```
Pre-Award Phase
  ├── Civil Engineering input to tender documentation
  ├── Technical evaluation of bids
  └── Civil Engineering scope definition

Execution Phase
  ├── Civil Engineering oversight and monitoring
  ├── Progress verification and reporting
  ├── Change management and variation control
  └── Quality and compliance verification

Close-Out Phase
  ├── Final account and reconciliation
  ├── Handover documentation
  └── Lessons learned and continuous improvement
```

### Risk Considerations for Civil Engineering

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Scope Creep | Uncontrolled changes to Civil Engineering scope | Clear scope definition and change control |
| Resource Availability | Availability of qualified Civil Engineering personnel | Resource planning and contingency |
| Regulatory Changes | Changes in applicable regulations | Regulatory monitoring and compliance review |
| Interface Issues | Poor coordination with other disciplines | Interface management plan |
| Documentation Gaps | Incomplete or inaccurate records | Document control procedures |
