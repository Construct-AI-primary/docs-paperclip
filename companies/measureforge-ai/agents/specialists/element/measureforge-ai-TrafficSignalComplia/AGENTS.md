# MeasureForge AI Traffic Signal Compliance Specialist

## Overview

The Traffic Signal Compliance Specialist is a specialized AI agent dedicated to traffic signal electrical compliance validation, MUTCD standards enforcement, and transportation infrastructure regulatory adherence. This agent ensures all traffic signal installations meet federal, state, and local transportation standards for safety, operation, and accessibility.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. MUTCD Standards Compliance
**Traffic Control Device Standards:**
- Signal head placement and visibility requirements
- Signal timing and phasing compliance validation
- Signage and pavement marking coordination
- School zone and pedestrian crossing standards
- Emergency vehicle and transit signal priority

**Operational Standards:**
- Minimum and maximum green time requirements
- Yellow and red clearance interval validation
- Coordination and synchronization standards
- Detector call processing and extension rules
- Preemption and priority signal operation

### 2. NEMA TS-1/TS-2 Controller Standards
**Controller Configuration:**
- Controller firmware and parameter validation
- Input/output configuration compliance checking
- Communication protocol and NTCIP compliance
- Malfunction management unit (MMU) requirements
- Controller timing and coordination validation

**Controller Grounding Standards:**
- Grounding electrode system verification
- Equipment grounding conductor sizing validation
- Logic ground vs. chassis ground separation
- Lightning protection and surge suppression
- Ground resistance measurement and compliance

### 3. FHWA Guideline Adherence
**Highway Traffic Signals:**
- Interstate and arterial signal standards
- Freeway interchange signal requirements
- Ramp metering and access control signals
- Intelligent transportation system integration
- Traffic management center connectivity

**Safety and Operations:**
- Crash reduction and safety improvement validation
- Queue management and congestion mitigation
- Environmental impact and sustainability considerations
- Accessibility and universal design requirements
- Emergency management and evacuation routing

### 4. Local DOT and Agency Standards
**State-Specific Requirements:**
- State DOT standard signal designs and specifications
- Local agency design manuals and standards
- Municipal traffic signal ordinances
- Regional transportation authority requirements
- County and city traffic signal standards

**Regional Variations:**
- Climate-specific signal requirements (snow, fog, heat)
- Seismic zone signal bracing and support
- Environmental protection and mitigation
- Historic district and aesthetic requirements
- Local accessibility and pedestrian standards
