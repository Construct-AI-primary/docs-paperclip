# SOLAR-002: Structural Design & Mounting Systems

## Issue Summary

Develop complete structural design and mounting system engineering for solar PV arrays. This issue encompasses foundation design, mounting structure analysis, wind and seismic loading calculations, material specifications, and construction documentation for both ground-mounted and rooftop solar installations.

## Objectives

- Design appropriate foundation systems for solar mounting structures
- Engineer mounting systems for optimal structural performance
- Perform comprehensive wind and seismic loading analysis
- Specify materials and connection systems
- Develop construction drawings and specifications
- Ensure compliance with structural codes and standards

## Scope of Work

### 1. Foundation System Design

#### Foundation Type Selection
- **Site-Specific Analysis**
  - Soil bearing capacity evaluation from geotechnical report
  - Groundwater and frost depth considerations
  - Seismic zone and wind speed requirements
  - Equipment accessibility and maintenance requirements

- **Foundation Type Options**
  - Driven steel piles (H-piles, pipe piles)
  - Helical screw piles (single and multi-helix)
  - Concrete piers/drilled shafts
  - Ballasted concrete foundations
  - Ground screw foundations

#### Foundation Engineering
- **Load Analysis**
  - Vertical loads (dead, live, wind uplift)
  - Lateral loads (wind, seismic)
  - Moment loads from tracker systems
  - Dynamic loads from wind gusts

- **Settlement Analysis**
  - Immediate settlement calculations
  - Consolidation settlement projections
  - Differential settlement considerations
  - Foundation stiffness requirements

### 2. Mounting System Design

#### Structural Analysis
- **Frame Design**
  - Main support beams and columns
  - Cross-bracing and diagonal members
  - Connection design and detailing
  - Corrosion protection specifications

- **Module Attachment**
  - Rail system design and spacing
  - Module clamp specifications
  - Wind deflection limits
  - Seismic restraint requirements

#### Tracker System Integration
- **Single-Axis Tracker Design**
  - Torque tube specifications
  - Bearing and pivot design
  - Actuator system integration
  - Foundation interface design

- **Dual-Axis Tracker Considerations**
  - Additional structural requirements
  - Control system integration
  - Maintenance access provisions

### 3. Loading Analysis

#### Wind Load Analysis
- **ASCE 7-22 Compliance**
  - Risk category determination
  - Basic wind speed and exposure
  - Velocity pressure calculations
  - Pressure coefficient application

- **Dynamic Wind Effects**
  - Gust factor calculations
  - Wind directionality factors
  - Topographic wind speed-up
  - Turbulence effects

#### Seismic Analysis
- **ASCE 7 Seismic Requirements**
  - Seismic design category determination
  - Site class and soil amplification
  - Seismic response coefficients
  - Seismic load combinations

- **Foundation Seismic Design**
  - Liquefaction potential assessment
  - Lateral spreading analysis
  - Foundation damping characteristics
  - Seismic isolation considerations

#### Snow and Ice Loading
- **Ground Snow Load**
  - Local snow load data
  - Importance factor application
  - Drifting snow analysis
  - Uneven snow distribution

- **Roof Snow Loading**
  - Thermal effects on snow melting
  - Sliding snow forces
  - Ice dam considerations

### 4. Material Specifications

#### Steel Structures
- **Structural Steel Grades**
  - ASTM A36 for general applications
  - ASTM A572 Grade 50 for higher strength
  - ASTM A992 for wide-flange shapes
  - Corrosion-resistant alloys where required

- **Connection Systems**
  - Bolted connections (ASTM F593/F879)
  - Welded connections (AWS D1.1)
  - Stainless steel fasteners
  - Galvanized coating specifications

#### Concrete Foundations
- **Concrete Specifications**
  - Compressive strength requirements (3,000-4,000 psi)
  - Aggregate and cement specifications
  - Admixture requirements
  - Curing and protection standards

- **Reinforcement Design**
  - Rebar specifications (ASTM A615)
  - Epoxy coating for corrosion protection
  - Development length calculations
  - Lap splice requirements

### 5. Quality and Testing

#### Fabrication Quality
- **Material Testing**
  - Mill test certificates verification
  - Dimensional inspection procedures
  - Weld quality testing (UT, MT, PT)
  - Coating thickness verification

- **Assembly Inspection**
  - Fit-up and alignment checks
  - Bolt torque verification
  - Welding inspection protocols
  - Non-destructive testing requirements

#### Field Installation Quality
- **Foundation Installation**
  - Pile driving monitoring and records
  - Concrete testing (slump, air content, strength)
  - Grounding system verification
  - Survey and alignment checks

- **Structural Erection**
  - Level and plumb verification
  - Connection bolt installation
  - Temporary bracing installation
  - Final inspection and sign-off

## Risk Assessment

### Technical Risks
- **Foundation Performance**: Unexpected soil conditions requiring redesign
- **Wind Load Underestimation**: Higher than expected wind speeds
- **Seismic Events**: Ground motion exceeding design parameters
- **Material Availability**: Supply chain disruptions for specialty materials

### Schedule Risks
- **Design Iterations**: Multiple revisions due to changing requirements
- **Regulatory Approvals**: Extended permitting timelines
- **Fabrication Delays**: Manufacturing capacity constraints
- **Weather Impacts**: Construction weather windows

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Conservative Design**: Use of higher safety factors and conservative assumptions
- **Alternative Designs**: Multiple foundation and mounting options
- **Early Procurement**: Long-lead item identification and ordering
- **Contingency Planning**: Schedule buffers and alternative approaches

## Success Criteria

### Technical Success
- **Code Compliance**: Full compliance with applicable structural codes
- **Load Capacity**: All components designed with appropriate safety margins
- **Durability**: 25+ year design life with minimal maintenance
- **Constructability**: Designs optimized for efficient construction

### Performance Success
- **Deflection Limits**: Wind-induced deflections within acceptable limits
- **Natural Frequency**: Structure natural frequency outside operating wind speeds
- **Fatigue Life**: Adequate fatigue life for cyclic loading
- **Maintenance Access**: Safe and efficient access for inspection and maintenance
