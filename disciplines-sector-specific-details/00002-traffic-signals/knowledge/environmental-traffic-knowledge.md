# Environmental Requirements for Traffic Signal Installations

## Overview

Traffic signal installations require environmental compliance to minimize impacts to natural resources, water quality, and community resources. This document covers the key environmental requirements for traffic signal installations, focusing on environmental impact assessment, stormwater management, erosion control, and permitting.

## 1. Environmental Impact Assessment

### NEPA Compliance
- **Categorical Exclusions**: Most traffic signal projects qualify for categorical exclusion
- **Environmental Assessment**: Required if categorical exclusion not applicable
- **Environmental Impact Statement**: Required for significant environmental impacts
- **Agency Coordination**: Early coordination with lead agency

### Impact Categories
- **Air Quality**: Temporary emissions from construction equipment
- **Water Quality**: Stormwater runoff during construction
- **Noise**: Construction noise impacts
- **Wildlife**: Impacts to wildlife and habitats
- **Cultural Resources**: Historical and archaeological resources

### Impact Mitigation
- **Air Quality**: Dust control, equipment maintenance
- **Water Quality**: Erosion control, stormwater management
- **Noise**: Equipment mufflers, work hour restrictions
- **Wildlife**: Timing restrictions, habitat avoidance
- **Cultural Resources**: Survey and monitoring

## 2. Stormwater Management

### Regulatory Framework
- **Clean Water Act**: Section 402 NPDES permit requirements
- **State Regulations**: State-specific stormwater regulations
- **Local Regulations**: Municipal stormwater requirements
- **Municipal Separate Storm Sewer System (MS4)**: MS4 permit compliance

### Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)
- **Required**: For construction activities disturbing >1 acre
- **Content**: Site description, controls, inspection, maintenance
- **Certification**: Signed by qualified personnel
- **Updates**: Updated as site conditions change

### Best Management Practices (BMPs)
- **Erosion Control**: Silt fence, straw bale barriers
- **Sediment Control**: Sediment basins, inlet protection
- **Stabilization**: Temporary seeding, mulching
- **Vehicle/Equipment**: Washout areas, spill prevention

### Post-Construction Stormwater
- **Runoff Control**: Minimize impervious surface increase
- **Detention**: If required by local regulations
- **Infiltration**: Promote infiltration where feasible
- **Maintenance**: Long-term maintenance plan

## 3. Erosion and Sediment Control

### Erosion Control Measures
- **Temporary Seeding**: Annual rye or fast-growing grasses
- **Mulching**: Straw or wood fiber mulch
- **Geotextiles**: Erosion control blankets
- **Contouring**: Maintain natural contours where possible

### Sediment Control Measures
- **Silt Fence**: Installed along perimeter
- **Inlet Protection**: Protect storm drain inlets
- **Sediment Traps**: Temporary sediment storage
- **Dust Control**: Water or dust palliatives

### Inspection and Maintenance
- **Frequency**: Minimum biweekly inspection
- **After Rainfall**: Inspect after rainfall events >0.5"
- **Repairs**: Repair damaged controls immediately
- **Sediment Removal**: Remove accumulated sediment

## 4. Wetlands and Waterways

### Regulatory Framework
- **Section 404**: Clean Water Act Section 404 permit
- **Section 401**: State water quality certification
- **State Regulations**: State wetland protection laws
- **Local Regulations**: Local wetland ordinances

### Site Evaluation
- **Wetland Delineation**: Field delineation by qualified professional
- **Waters of the US**: Determine jurisdictional boundaries
- **Buffers**: Identify required wetland buffers
- **Avoidance**: Avoid wetlands and waterways where possible

### Impact Minimization
- **Avoidance**: Route conduits to avoid wetlands
- **Minimization**: Minimize construction footprint
- **Mitigation**: Compensatory mitigation if impacts unavoidable
- **Restoration**: Restore temporarily impacted areas

## 5. Wildlife and Habitat Protection

### Endangered Species
- **Section 7**: Consultation for federally listed species
- **State Listings**: State-listed threatened and endangered species
- **Critical Habitat**: Critical habitat designations
- **Avoidance**: Avoid critical habitat and known species locations

### Migratory Birds
- **Migratory Bird Treaty Act**: Protection for migratory birds
- **Nesting Birds**: Avoid clearing during nesting season
- **Nest Surveys**: Surveys before clearing during nesting season
- **Timing**: Schedule clearing outside nesting season when possible

### Wildlife Corridors
- **Movement Patterns**: Consider wildlife movement patterns
- **Crossing Structures**: Consider wildlife crossing structures
- **Lighting**: Minimize light pollution to protect nocturnal wildlife
- **Fencing**: Wildlife-friendly fencing where required

## 6. Historical and Cultural Resources

### Section 106 Compliance
- **National Register**: National Register of Historic Places
- **Consultation**: SHPO and tribal consultation
- **Historic Properties**: Identify historic properties in project area
- **Effects**: Assess effects on historic properties

### Archaeological Resources
- **Phase I Survey**: Archaeological survey for project area
- **Phase II Evaluation**: Evaluation of identified resources
- **Phase III Data Recovery**: Data recovery if impacts unavoidable
- **Monitoring**: Archaeological monitoring during construction

### Cultural Resources
- **Sacred Sites**: Identify sacred sites and traditional cultural properties
- **Tribal Consultation**: Early tribal government consultation
- **Avoidance**: Avoid culturally significant areas
- **Treatment**: Develop treatment plan if impacts unavoidable

## 7. Environmental Permitting

### Federal Permits
- **Section 404 Permit**: Army Corps of Engineers permit
- **Section 401 Certification**: State water quality certification
- **Coastal Zone Management**: Coastal zone consistency if applicable
- **Floodplain**: Floodplain development permit if applicable

### State Permits
- **Stormwater Permit**: State stormwater discharge permit
- **Air Quality**: Air quality permits if applicable
- **Water Appropriation**: Water appropriation if dewatering required
- **Endangered Species**: State endangered species take permit

### Local Permits
- **Grading Permit**: Grading and excavation permit
- **Building Permit**: Building permit for signal structures
- **Right-of-Way**: Right-of-way permit for public property
- **Environmental**: Local environmental permits

## 8. Construction Environmental Controls

### Spill Prevention
- **SPCC Plan**: Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure plan
- **Secondary Containment**: Containment for fuel and chemicals
- **Spill Kits**: Spill response kits on site
- **Training**: Spill response training for personnel

### Waste Management
- **Hazardous Waste**: Proper disposal of hazardous waste
- **Construction Debris**: Recycling and disposal of construction debris
- **Sanitary Waste**: Portable toilets and proper disposal
- **Universal Waste**: Proper handling of universal wastes

### Vehicle/Equipment Maintenance
- **Washout Areas**: Designated washout areas
- **Fuel Storage**: Proper fuel storage and handling
- **Equipment Leaks**: Immediate repair of equipment leaks
- **Inspection**: Regular equipment inspection

## 9. Environmental Monitoring

### Construction Monitoring
- **Erosion Control**: Regular inspection of erosion controls
- **Stormwater**: Visual monitoring of stormwater discharge
- **Wildlife**: Monitoring for wildlife conflicts
- **Cultural Resources**: Monitoring during ground disturbance

### Post-Construction Monitoring
- **Vegetation**: Revegetation establishment monitoring
- **Stormwater**: Post-construction stormwater facility monitoring
- **Wetlands**: Monitoring of temporarily impacted wetlands
- **Restoration**: Monitoring of restoration areas

## 10. Documentation and Reporting

### Environmental Documentation
- **SWPPP**: Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan
- **Permits**: All permit applications and approvals
- **Inspection Reports**: Environmental inspection reports
- **Monitoring Data**: Environmental monitoring data

### Agency Reporting
- **NPDES Reporting**: Quarterly or annual DMRs
- **Section 404 Reporting**: Reporting per permit conditions
- **Incident Reporting**: Report environmental incidents to agencies
- **End of Project**: Notice of termination for NPDES permit
