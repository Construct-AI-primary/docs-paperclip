# Security Requirements for Traffic Signal Installations

## Overview

Traffic signal installations require comprehensive security measures to protect against physical threats, unauthorized access, and cybersecurity risks. This document covers the key security requirements for traffic signal installations, focusing on threat assessment, physical security, access control, and cybersecurity.

## 1. Threat and Risk Assessment

### Threat Categories
- **Physical Threats**: Vandalism, theft, sabotage, terrorism
- **Cyber Threats**: Hacking, malware, denial of service
- **Insider Threats**: Unauthorized access by personnel
- **Natural Threats**: Weather, natural disasters

### Risk Assessment Process
- **Asset Identification**: Identify critical assets
- **Threat Identification**: Identify potential threats
- **Vulnerability Analysis**: Analyze vulnerabilities
- **Risk Determination**: Determine risk levels

### Risk Mitigation
- **Avoidance**: Eliminate risks where possible
- **Reduction**: Reduce risk through controls
- **Transfer**: Transfer risk through insurance
- **Acceptance**: Accept residual risk

## 2. Physical Security

### Signal Cabinet Security
- **Locking**: High-security locks on all cabinets
- **Material**: Steel cabinets with tamper-resistant features
- **Reinforcement**: Reinforced door hinges and latches
- **Tamper Detection**: Tamper alarm on cabinet doors

### Pole Security
- **Anchor Bolts**: Security anchor bolts for poles
- **Grounding**: Grounding to prevent electrical tampering
- **Signage**: Warning signage for electrical hazards
- **Lighting**: Security lighting for nighttime visibility

### Detection System Security
- **Loop Security**: Loop lead-in protection
- **Camera Security**: Camera mounting security
- **Radar Security**: Radar unit mounting security
- **Wiring Protection**: Protected wiring for detection

## 3. Access Control

### Access Control Levels
- **Level 1**: Public access (sidewalks, crosswalks)
- **Level 2**: Authorized personnel (maintenance crews)
- **Level 3**: Technical personnel (engineers, technicians)
- **Level 4**: Administrative (managers, supervisors)

### Access Control Methods
- **Keys**: Physical keys for cabinets
- **Codes**: Access codes for electronic locks
- **Cards**: Access cards for electronic systems
- **Biometrics**: Biometric authentication for critical systems

### Access Control Management
- **Issuance**: Controlled issuance of access credentials
- **Tracking**: Track access credential usage
- **Revocation**: Immediate revocation of credentials
- **Auditing**: Regular access audits

## 4. Video Surveillance

### Camera Systems
- **Coverage**: Camera coverage of signal equipment
- **Resolution**: Minimum 1080p resolution
- **Storage**: Local and remote video storage
- **Retention**: Minimum 30-day video retention

### Camera Features
- **Night Vision**: Infrared capability for night operation
- **Weather**: Weatherproof housing for outdoor use
- **Analytics**: Video analytics for motion detection
- **Remote Access**: Secure remote viewing capability

### Camera Placement
- **Signal Cabinet**: Camera covering cabinet
- **Poles**: Camera covering pole bases
- **Detection**: Camera covering detection equipment
- **Access Points**: Camera covering access points

## 5. Alarm Systems

### Alarm Types
- **Tamper Alarms**: Cabinet and equipment tamper detection
- **Motion Alarms**: Motion detection in protected areas
- **Fire Alarms**: Fire detection in equipment enclosures
- **Temperature Alarms**: Temperature monitoring for electronics

### Alarm Response
- **Notification**: Immediate notification to monitoring center
- **Verification**: Verify alarm before dispatch
- **Escalation**: Escalate if response delayed
- **Documentation**: Document alarm events

### Alarm Integration
- **Central Station**: Integration with central monitoring station
- **TMC Integration**: Integration with traffic management center
- **Law Enforcement**: Direct connection to law enforcement
- **Emergency Services**: Connection to emergency services
