# QA/QC Requirements for Traffic Signal Installations

## Overview

Quality assurance and quality control are critical for traffic signal installations to ensure reliable, safe, and compliant systems. This document covers the key QA/QC requirements for traffic signal installations, focusing on inspection protocols, testing procedures, and documentation standards.

## 1. Quality Assurance Program

### QA Program Elements
- **Quality Policy**: Written quality policy statement
- **Organization**: Quality organization and responsibilities
- **Procedures**: Written procedures for quality activities
- **Training**: Quality training for personnel

### Quality Management System
- **ISO 9001**: Quality management system framework
- **Documentation**: Document control procedures
- **Records**: Quality records management
- **Audits**: Internal and external audits

### QA/QC Coordination
- **Roles**: Clear QA and QC role definitions
- **Responsibilities**: Defined responsibilities for each role
- **Communication**: Regular communication between QA and QC
- **Escalation**: Escalation procedures for quality issues

## 2. Inspection and Test Plans (ITPs)

### ITP Structure
- **Scope**: Project scope and specifications
- **References**: Applicable standards and specifications
- **Hold Points**: Inspection points requiring verification
- **Witness Points**: Points to witness but not hold

### ITP Content
- **Activity**: Description of activity to inspect
- **Criteria**: Acceptance criteria for activity
- **Method**: Inspection or test method
- **Records**: Required documentation

### ITP Implementation
- **Distribution**: Distribute ITP to all parties
- **Training**: Train inspectors on ITP requirements
- **Execution**: Execute ITP as work progresses
- **Updates**: Update ITP as needed

## 3. Material Verification

### Material Submittals
- **Submittal Schedule**: List of required submittals
- **Technical Data**: Product data sheets and specifications
- **Samples**: Physical samples where required
- **Certifications**: Manufacturer certifications

### Material Inspection
- **Visual Inspection**: Verify material condition on delivery
- **Identification**: Verify material identification and labeling
- **Documentation**: Verify documentation accompanies material
- **Storage**: Verify proper storage conditions

### Material Testing
- **Pre-Qualification**: Pre-qualify materials before use
- **Batch Testing**: Test materials by batch or lot
- **Field Testing**: Field testing where applicable
- **Laboratory Testing**: Laboratory testing for critical items

## 4. Pre-Installation Inspection

### Foundation Inspection
- **Excavation**: Verify excavation dimensions and soil conditions
- **Rebar**: Verify rebar size, spacing, and cover
- **Anchor Bolts**: Verify anchor bolt alignment and projection
- **Concrete**: Verify concrete placement and consolidation

### Conduit Inspection
- **Routing**: Verify conduit routing per plan
- **Bends**: Verify bend radii and sweep angles
- **Fittings**: Verify fitting weatherproofing and tightness
- **Pull String**: Verify pull string installation

### Equipment Inspection
- **Equipment Condition**: Verify equipment condition on delivery
- **Identification**: Verify equipment identification
- **Documentation**: Verify documentation accompanies equipment
- **Compatibility**: Verify equipment compatibility

## 5. Installation Inspection

### Civil Installation
- **Trench**: Verify trench dimensions and bedding
- **Backfill**: Verify backfill material and compaction
- **Pavement**: Verify pavement restoration
- **ADA Compliance**: Verify ADA-compliant pedestrian facilities

### Electrical Installation
- **Conduit**: Verify conduit installation
- **Wiring**: Verify wiring connections and terminations
- **Grounding**: Verify grounding system
- **Protection**: Verify protection devices

### Signal Installation
- **Poles**: Verify pole installation and alignment
- **Heads**: Verify signal head installation
- **Detection**: Verify detection system installation
- **Controller**: Verify controller programming

## 6. Testing Procedures

### Electrical Testing
- **Continuity**: Continuity testing of all circuits
- **Insulation Resistance**: Megger testing of insulation
- **Polarity**: Polarity testing of connections
- **Ground Resistance**: Ground resistance testing

### Signal Testing
- **Timing**: Verify signal timing parameters
- **Sequence**: Verify signal sequence
- **Detection**: Verify detection calls and extensions
- **Preemption**: Verify emergency preemption operation

### System Testing
- **Integration**: Test system integration
- **Communication**: Test communication systems
- **Monitoring**: Test monitoring systems
- **Alarms**: Test alarm systems

## 7. Commissioning Testing

### Pre-Commissioning
- **Checklist**: Complete pre-commissioning checklist
- **Documentation**: Verify all documentation complete
- **Punch List**: Address punch list items
- **Training**: Complete required training

### Commissioning
- **Controller**: Verify controller operation
- **Signal Heads**: Verify all signal heads operate
- **Detection**: Verify detection operation
- **Communication**: Verify communication to TMC

### Performance Testing
- **Timing Verification**: Verify timing meets specifications
- **Detection Verification**: Verify detection accuracy
- **Coordination**: Verify coordination with adjacent signals
- **Preemption**: Verify emergency preemption

## 8. Non-Conformance Management

### Non-Conformance Identification
- **Inspection**: Non-conformances identified during inspection
- **Testing**: Non-conformances identified during testing
- **Observation**: Non-conformances identified by observation
- **Customer**: Non-conformances identified by customer

### Non-Conformance Process
- **Identification**: Document non-conformance
- **Segregation**: Segregate non-conforming material
- **Investigation**: Investigate root cause
- **Disposition**: Determine disposition (use-as-is, repair, reject)

### Corrective Action
- **Root Cause**: Identify root cause
- **Corrective Action**: Implement corrective action
- **Verification**: Verify corrective action effectiveness
- **Documentation**: Document corrective action

## 9. Documentation Requirements

### Quality Records
- **Inspection Records**: Signed inspection records
- **Test Records**: Test results documentation
- **Non-Conformance Records**: NCR documentation
- **Training Records**: Training completion records

### As-Built Documentation
- **Drawings**: As-built drawings with changes marked
- **Wiring Diagrams**: Final wiring diagrams
- **Cable Schedules**: Final cable schedules
- **Test Results**: Final test results

### Project Closeout
- **O&M Manuals**: Operations and maintenance manuals
- **Warranties**: Warranty documentation
- **Training Records**: Training completion records
- **Project Completion**: Project completion documentation

## 10. Quality Audits

### Internal Audits
- **Schedule**: Annual internal audit schedule
- **Scope**: Audit scope covering all quality elements
- **Findings**: Document audit findings
- **Corrective Action**: Track corrective action to closure

### External Audits
- **Client Audits**: Client quality audits
- **Certification Audits**: ISO 9001 surveillance audits
- **Regulatory Audits**: Regulatory agency audits
- **Corrective Action**: Respond to audit findings

### Continuous Improvement
- **Metrics**: Track quality metrics
- **Analysis**: Analyze trends in quality data
- **Improvements**: Implement quality improvements
- **Lessons Learned**: Document and share lessons learned
