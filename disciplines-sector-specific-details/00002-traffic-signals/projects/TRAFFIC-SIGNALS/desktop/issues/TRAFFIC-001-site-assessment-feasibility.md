# TRAFFIC-001: Site Assessment & Feasibility

## Issue Summary

Conduct comprehensive site assessment and feasibility analysis for traffic signal installations. This critical first phase evaluates traffic conditions, intersection capacity, signal warrant compliance, and provides the foundation for all subsequent design and construction activities.

## Objectives

- Evaluate traffic volume and vehicle classification
- Assess intersection capacity and level of service
- Analyze signal warrant compliance per MUTCD
- Conduct preliminary geotechnical and foundation assessments
- Evaluate power supply and utility availability
- Prepare feasibility report with recommendations

## Scope of Work

### 1. Traffic Volume Analysis

#### Turning Movement Counts
- **24-Hour Counts**: Continuous vehicle count data collection
- **Turning Movements**: Right turn, through, left turn counts
- **Vehicle Classification**: Cars, trucks, buses, motorcycles
- **Peak Hour Analysis**: AM peak, PM peak, and weekend peaks
- **Pedestrian Counts**: Pedestrian volume at each crosswalk

#### Volume Characteristics
- **Average Daily Traffic (ADT)**: Daily traffic volume
- **Design Hour Volume**: 30th highest hourly volume
- **Growth Projections**: Historical data and future projections
- **Seasonal Variations**: Monthly and seasonal adjustments
- **Directional Distribution**: Split between directions

### 2. Intersection Capacity Analysis

#### Operational Analysis
- **Level of Service (LOS)**: Current LOS at each approach
- **Delay Analysis**: Average control delay per vehicle
- **Queue Length Analysis**: Maximum queue lengths
- **V/C Ratio**: Volume to capacity ratio
- **Critical Movement Analysis**: Identify constrained movements

#### Geometric Analysis
- **Lane Configuration**: Number and width of lanes
- **Turn Lane Storage**: Length of turn lanes
- **Intersection Sight Distance**: Visibility at intersection
- **Approach Grades**: Grades at intersection
- **Channelization**: Existing channelization features

### 3. Signal Warrant Analysis

#### MUTCD Warrants (Part 4)
- **Warrant 1 - Eight-Hour Vehicle Volume**
  - Minimum vehicular volume
  - Interruption of continuous traffic
  - 4-hour and 8-hour warrants

- **Warrant 2 - Four-Hour Vehicle Volume**
  - Four-hour vehicle volume threshold
  - Applicable conditions

- **Warrant 3 - Peak Hour**
  - Peak hour volume warrant
  - Delay on minor street

- **Warrant 4 - Pedestrian Volume**
  - Peak hour pedestrian volume
  - School crossing requirements

- **Warrant 5 - School Crossing**
  - School crossing signal warrants
  - Traffic control device requirements

- **Warrant 6 - Coordinated Signal System**
  - Progression considerations
  - System coordination benefits

- **Warrant 7 - Crash Experience**
  - Crash history requirements
  - Adequate trial of alternatives

- **Warrant 8 - Roadway Network**
  - Network justification
  - Traffic generators

### 4. Geotechnical Investigation

#### Soil Analysis
- **Boring and Testing Program**: SPT borings at foundation locations
- **Soil Classification**: Unified Soil Classification System
- **Groundwater Level**: Groundwater depth assessment
- **Corrosion Potential**: Soil resistivity and pH testing

#### Foundation Recommendations
- **Preliminary Foundation Type**: Selection based on soil conditions
- **Allowable Bearing Capacity**: Foundation support analysis
- **Frost Depth**: Frost penetration depth
- **Seismic Considerations**: Site class determination

### 5. Utility Assessment

#### Power Supply Evaluation
- **Available Capacity**: Utility transformer capacity
- **Service Voltage**: 120V or 208Y/120V availability
- **Utility Coordination**: Power company coordination
- **Backup Power**: UPS and battery backup requirements

#### Communication Assessment
- **Fiber Optic Availability**: Existing fiber network access
- **Cellular Coverage**: Cellular signal strength
- **Wireless Options**: Point-to-point wireless feasibility
- **ITS Integration**: Central management system connectivity

### 6. Environmental and Regulatory Review

#### Environmental Constraints
- **Wetland and Waterway**: Jurisdictional determinations
- **Historical Resources**: Cultural resource review
- **Wildlife Habitat**: Protected species review
- **Environmental Justice**: Community impact assessment

#### Regulatory Requirements
- **Local Permits**: Municipal permit requirements
- **State Permits**: DOT coordination and permits
- **Utility Permits**: Utility construction permits
- **Right-of-Way**: Right-of-way availability

## Deliverables

### Primary Deliverables

1. **Traffic Volume Report**
   - Volume data summary and analysis
   - Turning movement count summaries
   - Peak hour identification and analysis
   - Historical volume trends

2. **Capacity Analysis Report**
   - Operational analysis methodology
   - Current LOS analysis
   - Queue length calculations
   - Level of service projections

3. **Signal Warrant Analysis**
   - MUTCD warrant compliance matrix
   - Quantitative warrant analysis
   - Justification narrative
   - Recommendation for signal installation

4. **Preliminary Design Layout**
   - Concept layout showing lane configuration
   - Signal head locations
   - Pole locations
   - Right-of-way requirements

5. **Feasibility Study Report**
   - Technical feasibility assessment
   - Cost estimate range
   - Risk assessment and mitigation
   - Recommendation for project progression

### Supporting Documentation

6. **Geotechnical Summary Report**
   - Soil investigation results
   - Foundation recommendations
   - Construction considerations

7. **Utility Assessment Report**
   - Power supply availability
   - Communication options
   - Utility coordination requirements

8. **Environmental Review Memo**
   - Environmental constraints identified
   - Permitting requirements
   - Mitigation measures

## Technical Requirements

### Data Collection Standards
- **Traffic Counts**: ITE and DOT standards for count duration and equipment
- **Geotechnical**: ASTM standards for soil testing
- **Environmental**: NEPA and state environmental review standards

### Analysis Tools
- **Traffic Modeling**: Highway Capacity Software (HCS), Synchro, SimTraffic
- **Capacity Analysis**: HCM 6th Edition methodologies
- **Signal Timing**: VISSIM or equivalent microsimulation

## Quality Assurance

### Review Gates
- **Internal Review**: Technical accuracy and completeness
- **Client Review**: Alignment with project objectives
- **Regulatory Review**: Compliance with MUTCD requirements
- **Peer Review**: Independent technical validation

### Acceptance Criteria
- **Technical Accuracy**: All calculations verified and documented
- **Warrant Compliance**: Clear warrant analysis and justification
- **Regulatory Alignment**: All required permits identified
- **Economic Feasibility**: Realistic cost estimates
- **Schedule Alignment**: Feasible implementation timeline

## Dependencies

### Internal Dependencies
- **Project Kickoff**: Complete project initiation and scoping

- **Client Approval**: Project objectives confirmed
- **Team Assembly**: Key personnel assigned

- **DOT Coordination**: State DOT permit coordination

- **Local Agency**: Municipal approvals

## Risk Assessment

### High-Risk Items
- **Traffic Volume Changes**: Significant volume changes affecting warrant analysis
- **Utility Limitations**: Limited power supply capacity
- **Environmental Constraints**: Protected resources requiring mitigation
- **Right-of-Way**: Limited right-of-way for improvements

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Conservative Analysis**: Use conservative growth projections
- **Early Utility Contact**: Initiate power company coordination early
- **Environmental Screening**: Conduct early environmental review
- **Alternative Analysis**: Evaluate multiple improvement options

## Success Criteria

### Technical Success
- **Warrant Compliance**: Signal warrants met per MUTCD

### Business Success
- **Justified Investment**: Clear benefit-cost justification
- **Community Support**: Stakeholder alignment
- **Regulatory Approval**: Clear path to permits
- **Schedule Feasibility**: Realistic implementation timeline
