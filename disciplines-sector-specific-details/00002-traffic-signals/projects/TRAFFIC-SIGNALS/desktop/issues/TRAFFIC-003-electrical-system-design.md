# TRAFFIC-003: Electrical System Design & Power Integration

## Issue Summary

Design comprehensive electrical systems for traffic signal installations including power distribution, signal cabinet wiring, protection and coordination schemes, grounding systems, and utility interconnection. This phase ensures safe, reliable, and code-compliant electrical systems.

## Objectives

- Design power distribution system from utility to signal cabinet
- Design signal cabinet wiring and components
- Specify protection and coordination schemes
- Design grounding and bonding systems
- Coordinate with utility for service interconnection
- Prepare electrical construction documents

## Scope of Work

### 1. Power Distribution Design

#### Service Entrance Design
- **Service Voltage**: 120/240V single-phase or 208Y/120V three-phase
- **Service Capacity**: Minimum 100A with growth margin
- **Metering**: Utility revenue metering requirements
- **Disconnect**: Utility-grade disconnect switch

#### Power Supply Components
- **Main Circuit Breaker**: Sized per NEC 240.4
- **Surge Protection**: SPD per UL 1449
- **Transformer**: Utility or customer-owned per utility requirements
- **Grounding Electrode**: Per NEC Article 250

#### UPS and Battery Backup
- **UPS Sizing**: Based on signal load and runtime requirements
- **Battery Capacity**: Minimum 4 hours, 8 hours recommended
- **Transfer Time**: Instantaneous transfer specifications
- **Monitoring**: Remote monitoring requirements

### 2. Signal Cabinet Wiring Design

#### Cabinet Layout
- **Controller Location**: NEMA TS 1, TS 2, or ATC controller
- **Load Switches**: Number and type per phase requirements
- **Conflict Monitor**: NEMA TS 2 compliant monitor
- **Terminal Facilities**: Terminal block requirements

#### Wiring Design
- **Wire Specifications**: Size and type per circuit
- **Color Coding**: Phase identification colors
- **Termination Details**: Terminal block connections
- **Labeling**: Wire and terminal labeling

#### Component Specifications
- **Load Switches**: Solid-state or electromechanical
- **Flash Transfer Relay**: Flash mode transfer
- **Terminal Facilities**: Terminal block type and size
- **Auxiliary Equipment**: Detection, communication equipment

### 3. Protection and Coordination

#### Overcurrent Protection
- **Main Breaker**: Sizing for selectivity
- **Branch Breakers**: Individual circuit protection
- **Load Switch Protection**: Fuse or breaker per circuit
- **Coordination**: Proper coordination sequence

#### Ground Fault Protection
- **NEC 750.21**: Ground fault protection requirements
- **Equipment Ground Fault**: Enclosure protection
- **Signal Circuit Ground Fault**: Circuit protection
- **Coordination**: Coordination with utility protection

#### Surge Protection
- **SPD Types**: Type 1, 2, and 3 requirements
- **Installation Points**: Service, panel, controller
- **Rating**: Minimum 10kA for service entrance
- **Standards**: Per UL 1449

### 4. Grounding System Design

#### Grounding Electrode System
- **Ground Rods**: 8-foot minimum, 5/8" diameter
- **Concrete-Encased Electrode**: Ufer ground where available
- **Metal Water Pipe**: If accessible and usable
- **Resistance**: Maximum 25 ohms, 5 ohms preferred

#### Equipment Grounding
- **Grounding Conductors**: Sized per NEC Table 250.122
- **Bonding**: All non-current-carrying metal parts
- **Connections**: Compression or exothermic connections
- **Testing**: Fall-of-potential testing requirements

#### Signal Pole Grounding
- **Pole Ground Rods**: Ground rod at each pole
- **Counterpoise**: #4 AWG copper wire
- **Resistance**: Maximum 25 ohms per pole
- **Testing**: Individual pole ground testing

### 5. Utility Interconnection

#### Utility Coordination
- **Service Application**: Complete utility service application
- **Metering Requirements**: Revenue metering specifications
- **Protection Coordination**: Coordinate protection settings
- **Power Quality**: Utility power quality requirements

#### Service Requirements
- **Capacity**: Adequate capacity with growth
- **Reliability**: Consider dual-feed for critical signals
- **Accessibility**: Utility maintenance access
- **Clearances**: NESC clearances maintained

### 6. Construction Documentation

#### Electrical Plans
- **Power Plan**: Service entrance and distribution
- **Cabinet Wiring**: Complete cabinet wiring diagram
- **Grounding Plan**: Ground rod locations and connections
- **Conduit Plan**: Conduit routing for electrical

#### Specifications
- **NEC Compliance**: NEC Article 750 requirements
- **NEMA Standards**: NEMA TS 1, TS 2 requirements
- **Material Specifications**: Equipment specifications
- **Installation Specifications**: Installation requirements

#### Cost Estimate
- **Quantity Takeoff**: Electrical quantities
- **Equipment Costs**: Major equipment costs
- **Labor Costs**: Installation labor estimates
- **Total Estimate**: Complete project estimate
