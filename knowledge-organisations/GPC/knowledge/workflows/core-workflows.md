# GPC Guinea - Core Workflows

## Overview

GPC Guinea's main disciplines are civil engineering, building construction, and public works. For each, their primary workflows follow a structured project-cycle pattern typical of construction contractors.

## 1. Civil Engineering Workflows

**Typical Projects:** Road construction, bridge installation, drainage systems

### Primary Workflow Steps

#### 1.1 Project Scoping and Tendering

- Receive tender invitation or identify project opportunity
- Review project specifications and requirements
- Prepare cost estimates and tender submissions
- Submit bids for government or donor-funded projects

#### 1.2 Site Assessment and Planning

- Conduct site surveys and geotechnical assessments
- Assess access conditions and material availability
- Plan construction methodology and sequencing
- Prepare project schedule and resource plan

#### 1.3 Mobilisation and Setup

- Transport equipment and materials to site
- Establish site offices and accommodation
- Set up temporary utilities and facilities
- Brief construction teams on project requirements

#### 1.4 Earthworks and Ground Preparation

- Clear and grub site
- Excavate and shape terrain
- Install drainage and subgrade
- Compact and prepare foundation

#### 1.5 Structural Works

- Install sub-base and base course
- Construct road pavement or bridge structure
- Install drainage structures and culverts
- Complete earthworks and landscaping

#### 1.6 Finishing and Handover

- Apply surface treatments
- Install safety features and signage
- Conduct testing and quality checks
- Prepare as-built documentation
- Hand over to client

---

## 2. Building Construction Workflows

**Typical Projects:** Commercial buildings, public facilities, residential development

### Primary Workflow Steps

#### 2.1 Design Review and Planning

- Review architectural and structural designs
- Coordinate with engineers and consultants
- Prepare construction methodology
- Obtain necessary permits and approvals

#### 2.2 Site Preparation

- Clear and prepare site
- Set out building footprint
- Excavate foundations
- Prepare ground for construction

#### 2.3 Foundation and Structure

- Construct foundation
- Build structural frame (concrete, steel, or masonry)
- Install floor slabs and roof structure
- Complete structural works

#### 2.4 Building Envelope

- Install walls and cladding
- Complete roofing and waterproofing
- Install windows and doors
- Complete exterior finishes

#### 2.5 Internal Works

- Install electrical systems
- Install plumbing and mechanical systems
- Complete internal partitions and finishes
- Install fixtures and fittings

#### 2.6 Completion and Handover

- Conduct final inspections
- Complete punch list items
- Obtain occupation certificate
- Hand over to client

---

## 3. Public Works Workflows

**Typical Projects:** Municipal infrastructure, community facilities, government buildings

### Primary Workflow Steps

#### 3.1 Client Engagement and Specification

- Meet with government or municipal client
- Understand requirements and constraints
- Prepare scope of work
- Agree on specifications and timeline

#### 3.2 Procurement and Mobilisation

- Procure materials and equipment
- Mobilise construction teams
- Set up site facilities
- Commence construction

#### 3.3 Construction Execution

- Execute works according to specification
- Monitor quality and progress
- Manage site safety
- Coordinate with client representatives

#### 3.4 Quality Assurance

- Conduct regular inspections
- Test materials and workmanship
- Address defects and punch list
- Obtain client sign-off

#### 3.5 Project Close-Out

- Complete final documentation
- Train client on operation and maintenance
- Hand over project
- Obtain final payment

---

## 4. Cross-Disciplinary Project Management

Over all disciplines, a common management workflow runs in parallel:

### 4.1 Tender and Business Development

- Monitor tender opportunities
- Prepare competitive bids
- Develop client relationships
- Pursue repeat business

### 4.2 Project Planning and Control

- Develop project schedules
- Allocate resources and equipment
- Monitor progress and costs
- Manage risks and issues

### 4.3 Quality and Safety Management

- Implement quality management systems
- Ensure safety compliance
- Conduct regular audits
- Maintain certifications

### 4.4 Financial Management

- Track project costs
- Manage cash flow
- Invoice and collect payments
- Manage project profitability
